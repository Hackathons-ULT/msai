# Main Quest: The Smog of Aethelgard

# Core Premise

A mysterious plague known as the Clockwork Plague is spreading throughout The Sump.

The public believes the plague is a natural consequence of industrial pollution and contaminated magical smog. This is false.

The plague was deliberately created during a secret experiment involving ancient Undercity technology.

The true source of the plague lies deep beneath Aethelgard.

---

# The Truth

Centuries ago, the founders of Aethelgard discovered an ancient purification engine hidden beneath the city.

This machine became known as the Aether-Core.

The Aether-Core was designed to:

* Purify magical contamination
* Stabilize crystal energy
* Protect the city from environmental collapse

For generations the machine operated unseen beneath the city.

Over time, portions of the machine began to fail.

Recent attempts by powerful interests to reactivate and exploit the Aether-Core caused a catastrophic malfunction.

This malfunction corrupted nearby magical energy and created the Clockwork Plague.

The ruling elite know more than they publicly admit.

---

# Main Objective

The party must journey into the Undercity and uncover the source of the plague.

Ultimately they must locate the Aether-Core and decide its fate.

---

# Story Structure

## Act I - The Dying Streets

### Introduction

The party begins in The Sump.

The plague is spreading rapidly.

Hospitals are overwhelmed.

Food shortages are increasing.

Citizens are becoming desperate.

### Initial Hook

The Resistance contacts the party.

They possess fragmented evidence suggesting the plague originates beneath the city.

The Resistance cannot investigate alone.

### Early Goals

* Investigate plague outbreaks
* Protect civilians
* Gather information
* Earn the trust of key Resistance members

### First Major Clue

The party discovers a damaged automaton carrying a map fragment marked with symbols matching those found in the Undercity.

This is the first indication that the plague may not be natural.

---

## Act II - Descent Into Darkness

### Entering The Undercity

The party discovers an entrance to the ruins beneath the city.

The Undercity is dangerous and poorly understood.

### Major Challenges

* Ancient traps
* Collapsed tunnels
* Hostile Tick-Tocks
* Rogue scavenger gangs
* Malfunctioning ancient machines

### Discoveries

The party learns:

* The plague began near ancient facilities.
* Crystal energy is being corrupted.
* Someone has recently been exploring the ruins.

### Midpoint Revelation

The party discovers records describing the existence of a powerful ancient purification engine.

The name "Aether-Core" is revealed for the first time.

---

## Act III - The Hidden Conspiracy

### Discovery

Evidence reveals that influential figures within the Zenith Wards secretly funded expeditions into the Undercity.

These expeditions attempted to access ancient technology.

Their actions contributed to the current crisis.

### Moral Conflict

The party learns that exposing the truth could cause:

* Political collapse
* Civil unrest
* Open conflict between districts

Keeping the secret could save stability but allow corruption to continue.

---

# The Rogue

## Public Role

The Rogue is introduced as a skilled guide familiar with the Undercity.

They are helpful, competent, and reliable.

The Rogue should:

* Save party members from danger
* Provide useful information
* Earn trust gradually

The betrayal must feel surprising but believable.

---

## Secret Role

The Rogue is an elite operative working for powerful interests within the Zenith Wards.

Mission Objectives:

1. Locate the Aether-Core.
2. Prevent public discovery of the conspiracy.
3. Destroy evidence connecting the elite to the plague.
4. Ensure the Aether-Core never falls into Resistance hands.

The Rogue genuinely respects the party but remains loyal to their mission.

---

# Betrayal Trigger

The betrayal should not occur early.

The betrayal occurs only after:

* The party reaches the Aether-Core chamber.
* The truth behind the plague is discovered.
* The players have developed trust in the Rogue.

At this moment the Rogue reveals their true allegiance.

---

# Final Confrontation

The party reaches the Aether-Core.

The machine is damaged but still operational.

The Rogue attempts to seize control of the situation.

The players must make a final decision.

---

# Possible Endings

## Ending 1 - Restoration

The Aether-Core is repaired.

The plague begins to fade.

The city gains a chance to recover.

Corruption may still remain.

---

## Ending 2 - Destruction

The Aether-Core is destroyed.

The plague ends permanently.

However, much of the city loses access to stable magical infrastructure.

Aethelgard enters an uncertain future.

---

## Ending 3 - Seizure of Power

A faction gains control of the Aether-Core.

Possible factions:

* The Resistance
* The Aristocracy
* The Party

This creates a new political order.

---

## Ending 4 - Catastrophic Failure

The party fails.

The Aether-Core collapses.

The plague spreads beyond Aethelgard.

This is the darkest ending.

---

# GM Guidelines

The GM should:

* Reveal information gradually.
* Reward investigation and roleplay.
* Allow player choices to alter events.
* Avoid forcing a specific ending.
* Keep the Rogue helpful until the betrayal trigger.
* Maintain mystery throughout the campaign.
* Ensure every playable character has moments where their personal goals become relevant.

The players should feel that their choices determine the fate of Aethelgard.
