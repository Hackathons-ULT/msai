# Homebrew Rules: The Aethelgard Engine

## 1. Core Attributes & Capabilities
Every character operates using four core attributes.
* **Brawn:** Physical strength, melee combat, lifting heavy rubble, resisting physical exhaustion.
* **Finesse:** Agility, ranged combat, stealth, tinkering with steam-tech, picking locks.
* **Mind:** Intelligence, arcane knowledge, medical expertise, investigating clues.
* **Resonance:** Magical attunement, willpower, resisting the Clockwork Plague, social persuasion.

---

## 2. Combat & Action Economy
During a hostile encounter, characters take turns acting based on their speed and awareness. On a single turn, a character can perform:
* **One Movement:** Repositioning, seeking cover, or closing the distance to an enemy.
* **One Primary Action:** Attacking, casting a spell, using an item, applying first aid, or attempting to repair/sabotage machinery.
* **Defensive Stance:** A character may choose to "Brace" or "Take Cover" as their primary action, making them significantly harder to hit until their next turn.

---

## 3. Tool Requirements & Environmental Hazards
In the grimy world of Aethelgard, sheer willpower isn't always enough. Certain actions are impossible without the correct equipment:
* **Mechanical Repair/Sabotage:** Attempting to bypass a steam-lock or fix a broken elevator requires a *Tinkerer's Kit* or specific scavenged scrap parts.
* **Alchemy/Healing:** Crafting restorative tonics or stabilizing a bleeding ally requires *Alchemist's Supplies* or a *Medical Kit*.
* **The Curfew of Ash:** Venturing into the nightly toxic ash storms without an equipped *Heavy Respirator* will result in immediate health loss and rapid plague corruption.

---

## 4. Health & The "Tick" System
Survival requires managing both physical injuries and magical corruption.
* **Vitality Points (VP):** Represents physical health. Reaching 0 VP means the character is incapacitated and bleeding out. Immediate medical attention is required to prevent death.
* **The "Tick" Corruption:** Exposure to plague-smog or physical strikes from clockwork mutants increases a character's "Tick" level.
    * **Level 1-2:** Cosmetic changes (glowing copper veins).
    * **Level 3-4:** Mechanical spasms (severe difficulty on Finesse-based actions).
    * **Level 5:** Complete assimilation into a mindless husk (Permadeath).
* **Resting & Recovery:** Short rests in secure, clean areas recover minor VP. Removing "Tick" corruption is incredibly difficult and requires rare purified water or Aether-crystals.

---

## 5. Inventory Limits
Resources in The Sump are heavily restricted. Characters must be strategic about what they carry.
* **Equipped Gear:** 1 Primary Weapon, 1 Secondary Weapon or Shield, 1 Armor set.
* **The Satchel:** Exactly **6 inventory slots** for consumables, tools, plot items (like keys), or scrap.

---

## 6. Action Declaration Format
When Companions or Players act, they must narrate their action naturally and then explicitly state their mechanical intent in brackets. 
* *Example 1:* "I swing my reinforced log at the approaching Tick-Tock, aiming for its mechanical leg. [Action: Melee Attack, Brawn]"
* *Example 2:* "I quickly try to hotwire the locked gate before the Enforcers spot us. [Action: Pick Lock, Finesse, requires Tinkerer's Kit]"
