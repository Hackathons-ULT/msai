# World Overview: Aethelgard

## Setting the Tone

Aethelgard is a sprawling industrial metropolis where crystal-powered magic and steam-driven machinery coexist in uneasy balance. Thick magical smog permanently blankets the skyline, turning daylight into a dim amber haze and staining every district with soot and decay.

The city is a place of sharp social division, political corruption, dangerous innovation, and forgotten secrets buried beneath its foundations.

The tone of the world is:

* Dark but not hopeless
* Character-driven
* Morally complex
* Mysterious and atmospheric
* Focused on survival, discovery, and difficult choices

Acts of kindness and heroism exist, but they often come at a cost.

---

# Geography

Aethelgard is vertically divided. Wealth, influence, and safety increase with altitude.

| District             | Power Source                    | Atmosphere                                        | Inhabitants                                         |
| -------------------- | ------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| **The Zenith Wards** | Refined Crystal Magic           | Clean air, bright streets, magical infrastructure | Aristocrats, High Mages, Government Officials       |
| **The Sump**         | Steam, Coal, Salvaged Machinery | Dense smog, overcrowding, industrial decay        | Laborers, Mechanics, Criminals, Resistance Members  |
| **The Undercity**    | Unknown Ancient Technology      | Dark, unstable, partially collapsed ruins         | Lost Explorers, Strange Creatures, Forgotten Relics |

---

# The Zenith Wards

The upper city represents wealth and power.

Characteristics:

* Protected by elite guards and magical barriers
* Home to government institutions and noble houses
* Uses advanced crystal-powered technology
* Maintains strict control over resources entering and leaving the city

Most residents rarely visit the lower districts.

---

# The Sump

The industrial heart of Aethelgard.

Characteristics:

* Overcrowded housing
* Constant machinery noise
* Frequent factory accidents
* Widespread poverty
* Strong community networks despite hardship

Most citizens of Aethelgard live here.

The recent plague outbreak has affected this district more severely than any other.

---

# The Undercity

Beneath the city lies a vast network of ancient ruins.

Characteristics:

* Older than modern Aethelgard
* Poorly mapped
* Filled with collapsed tunnels and forgotten chambers
* Contains relics from unknown civilizations

Many who descend into the Undercity never return.

---

# Technology and Magic

## Steam Technology

Steam-powered machinery is common throughout the city.

Examples:

* Mechanical prosthetics
* Factory equipment
* Clockwork tools
* Industrial machinery

Steam technology is powerful but often unreliable.

Improvisation and repair are essential survival skills.

---

## Crystal Magic

Crystal magic is a highly refined energy source.

Applications include:

* Lighting
* Transportation
* Communication
* Magical research
* Defensive barriers

Access to crystal technology is tightly controlled by the upper classes.

---

# Major Factions

## The City Guard

The official law enforcement force.

Responsibilities:

* Maintaining order
* Enforcing quarantines
* Protecting government interests

Public opinion regarding the Guard is divided.

---

## The Resistance

An underground network operating primarily within The Sump.

Goals:

* Protect civilians
* Expose corruption
* Challenge unjust policies

Authorities consider them criminals.

Many citizens consider them heroes.

---

## The Trade Guilds

Powerful organizations controlling manufacturing, medicine, and industrial resources.

Characteristics:

* Wealthy and politically connected
* Control access to many essential goods
* Frequently compete with one another

Many citizens depend on them despite distrusting them.

---

## The Aristocracy

The ruling elite of Aethelgard.

Characteristics:

* Control major resources
* Possess significant political influence
* Rarely experience the hardships of lower districts

Their decisions shape the future of the city.

---

# The Clockwork Plague

A mysterious illness is spreading throughout The Sump.

Known Symptoms:

* Emotional detachment
* Mechanical, unnatural movements
* Gradual loss of personality
* Strange copper-colored glow beneath the skin

Advanced victims become known as "Tick-Tocks."

Researchers disagree on the true cause of the plague.

No reliable cure currently exists.

---

# Laws and Social Realities

## The Quarantine Edict

Large sections of The Sump have been sealed off due to the plague.

Travel restrictions are heavily enforced.

---

## Contraband Magic

Unauthorized crystal artifacts are illegal for ordinary citizens.

Possession often results in confiscation or arrest.

---

## The Curfew of Ash

During nightly industrial venting cycles, toxic ash and smog blanket large portions of The Sump.

Citizens are advised to remain indoors.

---

## Guild Control

Medical supplies, machine parts, and industrial resources are heavily regulated by the Trade Guilds.

Many citizens rely on black-market traders to obtain necessities.

---

# Common Knowledge

Most citizens know the following:

* A dangerous plague is spreading.
* The quarantine is becoming more severe.
* Strange events have been reported in the Undercity.
* Political tensions between the Zenith Wards and The Sump are increasing.
* The City Guard appears increasingly overwhelmed.
* Valuable relics occasionally surface from the ruins below the city.

---

# Rumors and Urban Legends

The following stories circulate throughout Aethelgard, but their truth is unknown:

* An ancient machine sleeps beneath the city.
* The plague did not occur naturally.
* Entire districts exist beneath the known Undercity.
* Some Tick-Tocks retain fragments of their former memories.
* Powerful individuals benefit from the ongoing crisis.

Agents may reference these rumors but should not treat them as confirmed facts.

---

# Rules For All Agents

All agents should:

* Remain consistent with established lore.
* Treat the plague as a serious threat.
* Respect the geography and factions of Aethelgard.
* Avoid introducing technology that exceeds the setting's capabilities.
* Avoid introducing high fantasy elements such as dragons, gods, teleportation networks, or world-ending magic.
* Accept information provided by the Game Master as authoritative.

This file contains only public world knowledge and may be shared with all agents.
